Purpose

InfoScape is a web app to help New Yorkers find useful living resources around them. Whether you're trying to find a cleaner neighborhood to move to, a place to get fresh produce, quality childcare, or even checking the safety of drinking water near your home or work, The app makes it easy to find information in one place. The app is simple to use and helps people make better choices for a better living standard.

Data Source and Key Features

The source of data for the app comes from NYC Open Data. I used the following datasets to create the first 4 key features in the app:

  • CleanCityScape - 311
  • NourishWellScape - NYC Farmers Markets
  • KidsCareScape - DOHMH Childcare Center Inspections
  • VivoWaterScape - Self-Reported Drinking Water Tank Inspection Results

I wanted the app to be easy for anyone to use, so I designed it with clear visuals and a simple layout. You can get to the information you need with just a few clicks, whether you're new to using data or more experienced.

Inspired by Service and Innovation, Built to Inspire

I got the idea to build the InfoScape from my time serving on the NYC Youth Leadership Council over the past two years. It made me realize how important it is to help people in my community and really understand what they need. I was also motivated by my studies in the Data Distinction program at school. It taught me how to think critically and analyze data, turning it from just numbers into meaningful storyline that can solve something for good. While working on the scapes, I became more aware of how to present data in easy ways for people to understand and actually helpful.

One of the most exciting parts was exploring the projects in the NYC Open Data Gallery. Seeing the technical documentation that others shared really pushed me to dive into building my own. It helped me every step of the way in shaping InfoScape this past summer. I appreciate the tremendeous teamwork behind the reliable and accessible free Data. It's fascinating to use the data for my project.

I’m a big believer in sharing knowledge, so I’m excited to share my technical journey through this app. By putting out documentation, I hope I can inspire others to create their own projects, make improvements, and use amazing data like NYC Open Data to make life better for everyone.
